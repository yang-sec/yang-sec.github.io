Course Description

Blockchain and distributed ledgers represent a new type of computer network that combines cryptography and distributed system techniques to realize decentralized decision-making amid zero or limited trust. This course aims to acquaint the students with selected aspects of blockchain and distributed ledger technologies that are currently actively researched or developed, with emphases on the blockchain core, security, decentralized application, and their intersections. The class format will be a mixture of lectures, student-led paper presentations, discussions, and a course project. Topics to cover include:

◾  Blockchain system architecture
◾  Consensus and fault tolerance
◾  Security, trust, and performance issues of blockchain core
◾  Blockchain network security
◾  New development in cryptocurrencies
◾  Smart contract and decentralized application
◾  Blockchain and data management
◾  Blockchain and trusted computing
◾  Blockchain application to other domains (IoT, supply chain, digital identity, etc.)

Student Learning Outcomes: By the end of the course, you are expected to develop an intermediate understanding of key technological and security problems facing blockchain systems as well as produce new results toward one specific problem. You will also gain valuable experience in reading technical papers and giving presentations in the research/professional setting.

Pre-req: Consent of instructor or two 500-level computer science courses.

Course Activities and Grading

(More detailed syllabus and announcements will be available in Canvas)

Class attendance and participation (10%): Students are required to attend every class. The attendance points (5%) will be given if a student attended more than 80% of the classes and did not miss more than 2 consecutive weeks. Students are also expected to actively participate in discussions in the classroom, evaluated by the participation points (5%).

Paper discussion online (20%): Students are expected to post on the Discussions board in Canvas about each of the papers covered in the coming classes. Please follow these guidelines:

◾  For the paper covered in Week X Day Y (X≥2), the discussions should be posted between Day Y 7:00PM of Week X-1 and Day Y-1 10:00PM of Week X. If you are the first to post on a paper, please start a new discussion thread titled Week X: Paper title (X is the paper’s week number) followed by a short summary of the paper’s main ideas. Other students can use this thread for posting on that paper.
◾  Each post should contain your own thoughts only, i.e., not repeating or rephrasing the posts made earlier by other students in the thread. Potential points to talk about include: paper contributions not yet mentioned by others, implementation/evaluation methodology, potential weaknesses, applicability to other scenarios, economic/ethical implications, more recent related work that you read, etc.

Paper presentation in class (20%): During each non-lecture class, one student will present one paper. Depending on the class size, each student will cover 1-3 papers throughout the semester. Please be advised to follow these guidelines when preparing a presentation:

◾  Create your own slides—do not reuse the slides from the original authors. Send the presentation slides (complete or near-complete) to xiaoy@uky.edu at least 3 hours before the class starts so the instructor can provide feedback.
◾  Limit the paper presentation to 30 minutes. Please include the following aspects: background, motivation, problem formulation, key solutions or analytical methods, implementation and evaluation, and future challenges per your understanding. Please also go over some of the discussion points that you found interesting during the online discussion.
◾  The remaining class time is dedicated to group discussion on the paper and relevant topics. The student presenter also joins the discussion and is expected to take brief notes on the key points mentioned by other students. Please upload the final slides and notes to Canvas (as an assignment) by the end of day.

Project: Students will explore a blockchain-related problem in groups of 1-2 people. It can be either tackling a new research problem, improving an existing solution/implementation, or a comparative study of different solutions. The following outcomes are expected:

◾  Proposal (5%): A 1-page write-up about your interested problem, objectives, and potential methodology.
◾  Mid-term presentation (10%): An in-class presentation about how your project proceeds, including problem formulation, related work, proposed method, and (optional) preliminary results. The duration depends on the number of groups—but be expected within 20 minutes.
◾  Final presentation (20%): A presentation about the final status of your project, including the components in the mid-term presentation except that the evaluation results are expected. Time and location will be determined near the semester end.
◾  Final report (15%): A project report (6 pages excluding references) that resembles a mini research paper, potentially publishable in a reputable venue after extension. Please use the ACM sigconf double-column LaTeX Template.

After the final percentage grade is calculated, the following scale is used to determine the final letter grade. For undergraduate students: 90-100% (A), 80-89% (B), 65-79% (C), 55-64% (D), 0-64% (E). For graduate students: 90-100% (A), 80-89% (B), 65-79% (C), 0-64% (E).

Policies

Academic Integreity: Please strictly follow the Academic Offenses Rules (regarding to plagiarism, cheating, and falsification or misuse of academic records). Also keep in mind that any copyrighted materials (e.g., images and other media), published contents (e.g., academic papers, books, web sources, online tools) used in your submissions and presentation slides should be properly cited. Ideas from people other than your own (for the project—ideas from outside your group) should be acknowledged.

Late Policy: Late submissions (paper discussion online, project proposal, project final report) will be subject to a 50% score penalty if you submit within 2 days after the deadline. Any submissions afterwards will be given the 0 score. Exceptions to this rule may be requested providing appropriate documentation (e.g., medical) which will be considered at the discretion of the instructor.
